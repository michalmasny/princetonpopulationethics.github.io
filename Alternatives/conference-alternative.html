Princeton Workshop on New Work in Population Ethics and the Ethics of Procreation

Overview:

This workshop will gather leading scholars in population and procreation ethics who will present their previously unpublished work.

Dates and place:

11-12 May 2019, University Centre for Human Values, Princeton University

Speakers:

Ralf Bader, University of Oxford

David Boonin, University of Colorado

Johann Frick, Princeton University

Hilary Greaves, University of Oxford

Elizabeth Harman, Princeton University

Jeff McMahan, University of Oxford

Melinda Roberts, The College of New Jersey

Tina Rulli, UC Davis

Samuel Scheffler, New York University

Larry Temkin, Rutgers University

*Talk titles and workshop schedule to be announced in due course.*

Accommodation:

The Nassau Inn, 10 Palmer Square, Princeton.

Travel infromation:

The nearest airport is Newark Liberty (EWR), but it is also possible to reach Princeton from the John F. Kennedy (JFK), LaGuardia (LGA), and Philadelphia (PHL) airports.

Passengers arriving at Newark Liberty (EWR) can take the AirTrain directly from the arrivals terminal to the Rail Link station to catch a New Jersey Transit Train to Princeton Junction. The one way cost is about $17.25. Note that neither the vending machines nor conductors accepts bills over $20.

To get to Princeton University from Pinceton Junction, you can take the free shuttle bus (scheduled to depart a few minutes after train arrival) or Uber/Lyft for about $10.

Speakers will stay at the Nassau Inn at 10 Palmer Square. It is a 10-15 minutes walk from the shuttle bus stop.

The workshop will take place in or near the Philosophy Deparment (exact venue TBC). It is located in the 1879 Hall. It is a 10 minutes walk from the shuttle bus stop and a 10 minutes walk from the Nassau Inn.
