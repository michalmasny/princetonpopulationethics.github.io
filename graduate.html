Graduate Conference on Population Ethics

In addition to the main event, we will host a satellite Graduate Conference on Population Ethics. It will take place on 10 May 2019 in Marx Hall, Room 301. The conference will feature presentations of work in progress by graduate students from Oxford University, Princeton University, and University of California, Davis.

Participation in the Graduate Conference is free and open to all.

Those planning to attend the main Workshop are warmly invited to participate in the Graduate Conference, but are in no way expected to do so.

Talk titles and the exact schedule will be announced in due course.

The conference is funded by the Philosophy Department of Princeton University. Unfortunately, we are not able to reimburse travel and accommodation costs of speakers and other participants.

Call For Abstracts

We are now accepting submissions from graduate students from Oxford University, Princeton University, and University of California, Davis. The topic is population ethics understood very broadly.

Submissions must be in the form of extended abstracts between 500 and 1000 words, suitable for a 30-minute presentation, fully anonymised, in PDF format, and sent to princetonpopulationethics@gmail.com by 23 March 2019.

We anticipate to inform submitters whether their paper was accepted within two days after the deadline.

Please direct all enquiries to princetonpopulationethics@gmail.com.
